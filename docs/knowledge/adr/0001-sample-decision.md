# ADR-0001: Sample — Decision title

> **This is a sample ADR** included with the template to show the expected shape. Replace it with your project's first real decision record, or delete it once you have one. New ADRs in your project should start from `0001-` (overwriting this file) or from `0002-` if you want to keep this as a reference.

## Status

Accepted — 2026-06-30

## Context

What problem were we trying to solve? What forces were in play (technical constraints, deadlines, team capacity, prior commitments)? Anyone reading this years later should be able to understand the situation without needing access to the original conversation.

Avoid restating things that are obvious from the codebase. Focus on the *forces* — the trade-offs and constraints — that made this a real decision rather than an obvious one.

## Decision

State the decision in one or two sentences, then expand with the specifics:

* What is being adopted, exactly.
* What is being explicitly rejected (alternatives that were considered and dismissed, with one-line reasons).
* The scope and boundary of the decision.

## Consequences

What this decision makes easy, and what it makes harder. Include:

* Operational consequences (what the team has to do differently).
* Migration / rollout implications.
* Known follow-ups or open questions that this decision intentionally defers.
* Cross-links to related ADRs, conventions, or runbooks: `[conventions/...](/docs/knowledge/conventions/...md)`.
